## 1. Purpose

This document defines the AutoMarket product backlog at Epic, Feature, and User Story granularity, so it can be imported into a ticketing system and worked sprint-by-sprint, in the order already fixed by the Sprint Backlog & Development Roadmap document.

It does **not** replace the Sprint Backlog document — that document still owns sprint sequencing and the Definition of Done. This document owns ticket-level decomposition within that sequence.

## 2. Review of the Draft Epic List

A 13-Epic draft was proposed, mapped one-to-one to the original 13 sprints, with an estimate of ~169 user stories for the MVP. The methodology shift (Sprint-only → Epic/Feature/Story/Task) is correct and the overall story-count order of magnitude is reasonable. Four corrections were necessary against the current document set:

| # | Issue in the draft | Why it matters | Source of correction |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Branch Management folded into "Vehicle Management" | Branch is sequenced **ahead of** Vehicle in the reconciled Sprint Backlog specifically because Vehicle/Reservation/Offer/TestDrive all carry a denormalized `branch_ID` from creation (AD-38). Treating it as a Vehicle sub-topic reintroduces the exact sequencing bug the Sprint Backlog reconciliation fixed. | Sprint Backlog §7, Solution Architecture AD-38 |
| 2 | Pricing not represented as an Epic at all | Pricing is a first-class domain with its own CDS file, own service (`PricingService`), own event (`VehiclePriceDropped`), and its own Sprint (4). It is not a sub-feature of Vehicle or Favorites. | Domain Model §8, OData Service Design §7, Sprint Backlog §8 |
| 3 | Guest Checkout absent from the "Reservations" Epic's topic list | Guest Checkout (`guestToken`, `claimReservation`, guest-scoped rate limiting) is the single most-revised decision across the entire document set (AD-39/AD-40). Omitting it from the Epic/Feature breakdown risks it being silently dropped again when the backlog is elaborated into tickets — which is exactly what happened to the original 7 docx documents before reconciliation. | Solution Architecture AD-39/AD-40, Domain Model §10, Authorization Matrix §6 |
| 4 | Concurrency Handling left under "Sales & Checkout" (Sprint 8 in the original draft) | This is the specific sequencing risk the Sprint Backlog reconciliation closed: the "one active reservation per vehicle" invariant is introduced in Sprint 5 (Reservation), not Sprint 8 — leaving it there reopens a 3-sprint window with no DB-level enforcement. | Sprint Backlog §9, §20 (Risks) |
| 5 | Data & Compliance (DPIA, data classification, migration runbook) folded into generic "Production Readiness" | The Sprint Backlog deliberately split this into its own Sprint 13 because DPIA/governance sign-off is a hard compliance gate, not a generic hardening checklist item that can slip. | Sprint Backlog §17 |

Two smaller, non-structural notes: MFA/lockout/rate-limiting deserve their own stories under Identity & Security rather than being implied by a generic "Authentication" topic (they carry specific, testable numbers — 5 attempts/15 min, 30-min lockout — that should appear in acceptance criteria, not just prose); and the Payment Epic's topic list ("Payment Status Tracking") should explicitly call out the two-terminal-branch state machine (FAILED/REFUNDED as separate branches, not one chain) so a story-writer doesn't quietly rebuild the exact linear-chain mistake the Solution Architecture Document's first review pass found and corrected.

**Version 1.1 note:** Branch Management and Vehicle Management were implemented in a single sprint (Sprint 3) and are tracked as one combined Epic (EPIC-03) in the sprint doc and git history. Pricing and Favorites were similarly combined into EPIC-04 (Sprint 4). The Epic list below reflects this implementation numbering; user story IDs (US-3.x for Branch, US-4.x for Vehicle, US-5.x for Pricing, US-6.x for Favorites) are unchanged and remain valid cross-references.

The story-count estimate changes accordingly — see Section 3.

## 8. EPIC-05 — Reservations & Guest Checkout (Sprint 5)

**Features**: Reservation Lifecycle · Concurrency & Uniqueness Enforcement · Guest Checkout & Claim Flow · Reservation Expiry

This Epic explicitly absorbs **Concurrency Handling** — moved here from the original "Sales" placement because the "one active reservation per vehicle" invariant is introduced at this point in the build, not three sprints later (Sprint Backlog §9, §20).

**Reservation Lifecycle**

* As a Customer, I want to create a reservation on a `FOR_SALE` vehicle.
* As an Operator/Manager, I want to approve a reservation.
* As an Operator/Manager, I want to reject a reservation.
* As a Customer, I want to cancel my own reservation.
* As an Operator/Manager, I want to complete a reservation once inspection/checkout is done.
* As the system, I want a cancelled/expired reservation to emit `VehicleReleased` and return the vehicle to `FOR_SALE`.

**Concurrency & Uniqueness Enforcement**

* As the system, I want a partial unique DB index (`UNIQUE(vehicle) WHERE status IN ('REQUESTED','APPROVED')`), so only one active reservation can ever exist per vehicle, even under a guard-logic bug.
* As the system, I want a `SELECT...FOR UPDATE` row lock taken on the vehicle before evaluating the reservation guard.
* As a QA engineer, I want a load test firing concurrent reservation requests at the same vehicle, asserting exactly one succeeds.

**Guest Checkout & Claim Flow**

* As a Guest, I want to create a reservation without logging in.
* As the system, I want to issue a signed `guestToken` on guest-created reservations instead of a `customer_ID`.
* As a Guest, I want to view or cancel my own reservation by presenting my `guestToken`.
* As a Guest, I want to be prompted to claim my reservation at checkout.
* As a Guest, I want a `claimReservation` action that converts my guest reservation into an identified-customer reservation once I log in or register.
* As the system, I want `ReservationClaimed` emitted on claim, setting `customer_ID` and clearing `guestToken`.
* As the system, I want guest reservation writes rate-limited at 20 req/min per IP.
* As an Operator/Manager, I want to approve/reject reservations regardless of guest- or customer-origin, scoped only by branch.

**Reservation Expiry**

* As the system, I want a periodic job to scan reservations past `createdAt + 48h` and fire `ReservationExpired`.
* As the system, I want the 48-hour window anchored to the original creation timestamp, never reset by a failed checkout attempt.
* As a Customer/Guest, I want to be notified when my reservation is about to expire or has expired.
* As a developer, I want an end-to-end test for "guest reservation → abandon → return later → claim → checkout" across a session gap.

## 11. EPIC-08 — Sales & Checkout (Sprint 8)

**Features**: Checkout Flow · Order Creation & Uniqueness · Cross-Aggregate Choreography (Checkout Saga)

* As a Customer, I want to start checkout on a `FOR_SALE` or `RESERVED` vehicle I own (or have just claimed), creating an Order.
* As the system, I want `CheckoutStarted` to move the vehicle to `PENDING_PAYMENT` and emit `VehicleCheckoutStarted`.
* As the system, I want a partial unique index on Orders, as defense in depth alongside the Reservation-level guard.
* As a Customer, I want to choose a delivery type (pickup/home delivery) at checkout.
* As a Customer, I want to view an order summary before confirming.
* As a Customer, I want to cancel my order before payment completes.
* As a Manager, I want to mark an order completed after delivery.
* As the system, I want a direct-purchase checkout (no prior reservation) to go straight `FOR_SALE`→`PENDING_PAYMENT`, skipping `RESERVED` legitimately.
* As the system, I want the Order aggregate to react to `PaymentSucceeded`/`PaymentFailed` as choreography, not a central saga engine.
* As the system, I want the Vehicle aggregate to react to the same payment events independently of Order's own reaction.
* As a developer, I want the Checkout choreography documented and integration-tested explicitly, so it's never built as a hidden implicit coupling.
* As a Customer, I want to view my order history.
* As a Manager, I want to view orders scoped to my branch.
* As the system, I want checkout from `RESERVED` to validate the reservation is still within its 48-hour window, rejecting otherwise.

* As the system, I want `CheckoutStarted` rejected if the requester is not the reservation/offer owner (or hasn't just claimed a guest reservation).
* As a developer, I want an idempotency key on the checkout request itself, distinct from the Payment-level key.
* As a Manager, I want to cancel an order on the customer's behalf before payment.

## 12. EPIC-09 — Payments (Sprint 9)

**Features**: PSP Integration & Tokenization · Payment State Machine · Idempotency · Refunds

* As a Customer, I want to pay via a PSP-hosted page/redirect, so my card data never touches AutoMarket's servers.
* As the system, I want `initiatePayment` to require a client-generated idempotency key, rejecting the request if absent.
* As the system, I want the Payment state machine enforced as `INITIATED→AUTHORIZED→CAPTURED` with `FAILED`/`REFUNDED` as **separate terminal branches** — never one linear chain.
* As the system, I want webhook signature verification with replay protection (timestamp + nonce) on all inbound PSP callbacks.
* As a Customer, I want to retry a failed payment.
* As a Manager, I want to issue a full or partial refund on a captured payment.
* As the system, I want a refund possible only from `CAPTURED` — never from `FAILED`.
* As a Customer, I want to view my payment status and history.
* As the system, I want `PaymentFailed` to trigger the Vehicle/Order choreography reactions, so the vehicle isn't stuck in `PENDING_PAYMENT`.
* As the system, I want only a PSP token + last4 + brand stored — never PAN/CVV — keeping PCI-DSS scope at SAQ-A.
* As a developer, I want a PSP integration POC completed before the sprint depends on it.
* As a QA engineer, I want idempotency tested under simulated PSP retries (duplicate webhook delivery).
* As the system, I want `transactionReference` (PSP's own reference) stored, distinct from the internal idempotency key.
* As an Admin/Manager, I want captured payment data immutable — no role or action edits it.
* As a developer, I want the PSP vendor decision (Stripe/Adyen/SAP Multi-Bank Connectivity) finalized before this sprint starts.

## 18. Technical Task Decomposition — Worked Example

Tasks are deliberately **not** pre-created for all 194 stories — that level of detail belongs to sprint-refinement sessions held immediately before each sprint, not to this document. One Phase-1 story is decomposed below to illustrate the pattern a refinement session should follow:

**US-4.11** — *As the system, I want a `SELECT ... FOR UPDATE` row lock taken before any state-transition guard evaluates.*

* TASK-4.11.1 — Add `FOR UPDATE` clause to `VehicleRepository.findForTransition(vehicleId)`.
* TASK-4.11.2 — Wrap the guard-evaluation + state-write in a single DB transaction.
* TASK-4.11.3 — Write a concurrency integration test: two parallel transition requests against the same vehicle ID, assert exactly one commits.
* TASK-4.11.4 — Add a metric/log line on lock-wait time, so contention is observable in production.

A typical story decomposes into 2-5 tasks. At 194 stories, this implies roughly 400-900 tasks over the life of the project — consistent with the original order-of-magnitude estimate, generated progressively rather than upfront.
