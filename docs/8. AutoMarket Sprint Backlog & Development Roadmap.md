This revision fixes a sequencing risk found during reconciliation: **Concurrency Handling** had been placed in Sprint 7 (Sales) even though the "only one active reservation per vehicle" invariant is needed as early as Sprint 4 (Reservations) — for three sprints, that invariant would have had no DB-level enforcement. It also adds Branch and Pricing as their own sprint deliverables, pulls MFA/lockout/rate-limiting into Sprint 1 where they belong, adds the Guest Checkout work explicitly to Sprint 4, and adds a Data & Compliance sprint before hardening.

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines: MVP scope, sprint planning, development order, module dependencies, release roadmap. The roadmap follows a business-value-first strategy.

---

## 2. MVP Definition

**Customer Features**: Vehicle Search, Vehicle Detail, Favorites, Reservation (including Guest Checkout), Test Drive Request (including Guest), Offer Submission, Vehicle Purchase, Payment, Notifications.

**Internal Features**: Vehicle Management, Branch Management, Pricing/Price History, Reservation Management, Offer Management, Sales Management.

**Platform Features**: Authentication (incl. MFA/lockout), Authorization (RBAC+ABAC), Rate Limiting, Audit Logging.

## 6. Sprint 1 — Identity & Security

**Identity**: User Entity, Role Entity, User Role Assignment.

**Authentication**: Local Authentication, JWT Support, Password Hashing (Bcrypt).

**Authentication Hardening** *(new — previously scattered or missing)*:

* MFA enforcement: mandatory for Operator/Manager/Admin, risk-based for Customer.
* Account lockout: 5 failed attempts / 15 min → 30-min lockout.
* Session idle timeout config at the Approuter: Admin 15 min, Manager/Operator 30 min.
* Rate limiting at the Approuter: 100/300 req-min authenticated, 20/100 req-min guest (the guest tier has no traffic to protect yet this sprint, but the Approuter config must exist before Sprint 4 introduces guest writes).

**XSUAA**: Role Collections, Role Mapping.

**Admin Portal**: Login, User List.

**Deliverables**: Identity Module, Authentication Module, Authorization Framework (RBAC scaffold).

---

## 7. Sprint 2 — Branch & Vehicle Management

**Branch Domain** *(new — moved ahead of Vehicle)*: Branch CRUD, Region field, active/inactive status.

**Vehicle Domain**: Vehicle CRUD, Vehicle Images, Vehicle Status Management (full state table from the Implementation Architecture Document, not a simplified linear version), `modifiedAt`-backed optimistic locking (`@odata.etag`).

**Vehicle Service**: Vehicle API, Vehicle Search, **cache-bust wiring for `VehicleSold`/`VehiclePublished`/`VehicleReserved`/`VehicleReleased`/`VehicleCheckoutStarted`** *(new)*.

**Operator Portal**: Vehicle List, Vehicle Detail, Create Vehicle.

**Customer Portal**: Vehicle Catalog, Vehicle Detail (guest-accessible from this sprint on).

**Deliverables**: Branch Module, Vehicle Module, Vehicle Portal Screens.

---

## 8. Sprint 3 — Pricing & Favorites

**Pricing Domain** *(new)*: `PriceHistory` entity, `VehiclePriceDropped` event on price decrease, Offer-vs-list-price comparison function.

**Favorites**: Add Favorite, Remove Favorite, Favorite List, unique constraint (customer + vehicle).

**Customer Portal**: Favorites Page, price-history sparkline on Vehicle Detail.

**Deliverables**: Pricing Module, Favorites Module, Favorites UI.

---

## 9. Sprint 4 — Reservations, Guest Checkout & Concurrency

**Concurrency Handling moved here from Sprint 7** — this is the sprint where the "only one active reservation per vehicle" invariant is introduced, so it must be enforced at the DB level from day one, not three sprints later:

* Partial unique index: `UNIQUE (vehicle) WHERE status IN ('REQUESTED','APPROVED')`.
* `SELECT ... FOR UPDATE` row lock in the repository before the domain layer evaluates the transition guard.

**Reservation Domain**: Reservation Creation, Approval Workflow, Cancellation Workflow, **48-hour validity window + expiry poller** *(new)*.

**Guest Checkout** *(new)*: `guestToken` issuance on unauthenticated `createReservation`, `claimReservation` action, guest read/cancel via token possession, guest write rate limiting (20 req/min/IP) actually wired (config existed since Sprint 1, traffic starts now).

**Customer Portal**: Reservation Form (guest-capable), Reservation Status, Claim Reservation banner.

**Operator Portal**: Reservation Approval (branch-scoped via denormalized `branch_ID`).

**Deliverables**: Reservation Module, Guest Checkout flow, Reservation UI.

---

## 10. Sprint 5 — Test Drive Management

**Test Drive Domain**: Test Drive Request (guest-capable via inline contact capture, no claim step needed), Time Slot Validation, Availability Check.

**Customer Portal**: Test Drive Scheduler (guest-capable).

**Operator Portal**: Test Drive Management.

**Deliverables**: Test Drive Module, Scheduling Engine.

---

## 11. Sprint 6 — Offer Management

**Offer Domain**: Offer Submission (authenticated only — no guest path), Offer Approval (creates a Reservation that inherits the Offer's identity directly), Offer Rejection, resubmission of rejected offers.

**Customer Portal**: Offer Form, Offer Tracking.

**Manager Portal**: Offer Management.

**Deliverables**: Offer Module, Approval Workflow.

---

## 12. Sprint 7 — Online Vehicle Purchase

**Sales Domain**: Order Creation, **Order-level uniqueness constraint** (`UNIQUE (vehicle) WHERE status IN ('CREATED','PENDING_PAYMENT','PAID')` — defense in depth alongside the Vehicle-level guard, not a replacement for the Sprint 4 reservation locking work).

**Cross-aggregate choreography** *(new)*: `CheckoutStarted` → Payment state machine → `PaymentSucceeded`/`PaymentFailed` → Vehicle/Order react. Documented and tested as choreography, not built as a hidden implicit coupling.

**Customer Portal**: Checkout, Order Summary.

**Deliverables**: Sales Module, Checkout Flow.

---

## 13. Sprint 8 — Payments

**Payment Domain**: Payment Provider Integration (PSP-hosted tokenization — no PAN ever touches AutoMarket servers), Idempotency Handling, Payment Status Tracking (`Initiated → Authorized → Captured`, with `Failed`/`Refunded` as separate terminal branches, not a single chain).

**Customer Portal**: Payment Page (PSP redirect/hosted fields only), Payment History.

**Deliverables**: Payment Module, Online Payment Flow.

---

## 14. Sprint 9 — Notifications

**Notification Domain**: Email Notifications, Retry Mechanism, Template Engine.

**Favorites-driven triggers** *(new)*: `VehiclePriceDropped`, `VehicleSold`, `SimilarVehicleListed` (attribute-equality matching only — no preference engine).

**Events consumed**: Reservation Approved, Offer Approved, Vehicle Sold, Test Drive Cancelled, plus the three above.

**Deliverables**: Notification Module, Email Engine, Favorites re-engagement triggers.

---

## 15. Sprint 10 — Delivery Management

**Delivery Domain**: Delivery Scheduling, Delivery Tracking.

**Manager Portal**: Delivery Dashboard.

**Deliverables**: Delivery Module.

---

## 16. Sprint 11 — Reporting

**Reporting Domain**: populated from the transactional outbox, never live operational tables — retention independent of the outbox's 2-year window.

**Reports**: Vehicle Sales, Reservation Conversion, Offer Conversion, Branch Performance, **dual funnel conversion (direct vs. reservation-led, reported separately)** *(new)*.

**Manager Portal**: Reports Dashboard.

**Deliverables**: Reporting Module.

---

## 17. Sprint 12 — Data & Compliance Hardening *(new — split out of the original single hardening sprint)*

**Data**:

* Data Classification tiers applied across all entities (CDS Data Model Section 21).
* Legacy data migration & cutover plan executed branch-by-branch, with a reconciliation report per branch before its legacy system is decommissioned.
* Audit/Domain Event/Notification Log retention jobs verified against the 7y/2y/1y policy.

**Compliance**:

* DPIA (GDPR Art. 35) completed and signed off by the Data Protection Officer — a hard gate, not a checklist item that can slip.
* Governance sign-off collected: Architecture, Security, DPIA, Business — each a role, checked against its corresponding document.

**Deliverables**: Migration runbook, signed DPIA, populated Data Classification register.

---

## 18. Sprint 13 — Hardening & Production Readiness

**Security**: Penetration Testing, Vulnerability Scan, Threat Model (STRIDE) re-validated against what was actually built.

**Performance**: Load Testing against the per-hop latency budget (Approuter ≤50ms, CAP handler ≤150ms, HANA query ≤200ms, network ≤100ms), Stress Testing at 2x the assumed peak (400 req/s) — flagged as a placeholder until a real traffic projection replaces it.

**Reliability**: Error budget burn-rate alerting configured against the 99.5% SLO (fast burn 5%/1h, slow burn 10%/6h), on-call escalation runbook published.

**Chaos**: Outbox dispatch poller killed mid-batch in a non-prod environment to verify at-least-once delivery and consumer idempotency hold under failure.

**Quality**: Regression Testing, UAT Support.

**Operations**: Monitoring Validation, Backup Validation, Disaster Recovery Validation (RPO ≤ 1h, RTO ≤ 4h).

**Deliverables**: Production Ready Release.

## 19. Definition of Done

A backlog item is considered complete when:

* Development completed.
* Code reviewed.
* Unit tests passed — **80% coverage on domain/business-logic modules, 60% overall**, enforced as a CI gate, not just measured.
* Integration tests passed.
* Contract tests passed (event payload schema validated against the catalog).
* Security validation completed.
* Documentation updated.
* CI/CD pipeline passed.

## 20. Risks

| Risk Area | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Payment Integration | Early POC with the chosen PSP; idempotency tested under simulated retries |
| Concurrency Control | Moved to Sprint 4 (was Sprint 7) — load-tested for the reservation race window specifically, not just generically |
| Reservation Conflicts | Partial unique index + `FOR UPDATE`, tested under concurrent load |
| Guest-to-Customer Claim Flow | E2E test: guest reservation → abandon → return later → claim → checkout, across a session gap |
| Notification Deliverability | Contract testing, retry mechanism |
| Cache Invalidation Completeness | Explicit test asserting every Vehicle status transition (both directions) busts the catalog cache |

## 22. Success Criteria

Release 1.0 is considered successful when:

* Customers (and guests, where permitted) can purchase vehicles online.
* Customers and guests can reserve vehicles, with the claim flow working across a session gap.
* Customers can submit offers (authenticated).
* Customers and guests can schedule test drives.
* Internal users can manage inventory, scoped correctly to their branch.
* Payment flow is operational with no card data ever touching AutoMarket servers.
* Notifications, including the three Favorites-driven triggers, are operational.
* The reservation/order concurrency invariants hold under load test, not just in the happy path.
